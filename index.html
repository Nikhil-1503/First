Overview

Coronaviruses are a family of viruses that can cause illnesses such as the common cold, severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS) and Middle East respiratory syndrome (MERS). In 2019, a new coronavirus was identified as the cause of a disease outbreak that originated in China.

The virus is now known as the Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). The disease it causes is called Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19). In March 2020, the World Health Organization (WHO) declared the COVID-19 outbreak a pandemic.

Symptoms

Signs and symptoms of COVID-19 may appear two to 14 days after exposure and can include:

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty in breathing

    • Other symptoms can include:

  • Fatigue
  • Aches
  • Runny nose and/or sneezing
  • Sore throat
  • Persistent chest pain

    • More rarely, the disease can be serious and even fatal. Older people, and people with other medical conditions (such as asthma, diabetes, or heart disease), may be more vulnerable to becoming severely ill.

    Prevention

    You can protect yourself and help prevent spreading the virus to others if you:

  • Wash your hands regularly for atleast 20 seconds, with soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub
  • Cover your nose and mouth with a disposable tissue or flexed elbow when you cough or sneeze
  • Avoid close contact (2 meter or 6 feet) with people who are unwell
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, or mouth
  • Avoid large events and mass gatherings.
  • Clean and disinfect high-touch surfaces daily.
  • Use masks in public

    • Treatment

    There is no specific medicine to prevent or treat coronavirus disease (COVID-19).
